Street Fighter V

Street Fighter V is a fighting game published by Capcom, who co-developed the game with Dimps.

What is it about?

Street Fighter V retains the side-scrolling fighting game style of its predecessors, in which two fighters use a variety of attacks and special abilities to knock out their opponent. The game features the EX meter introduced in Street Fighter III, which increases as the player connects his attacks to the opponent and can be used either to increase the power of his special attacks or to perform super combos known as Critical Arts, although In this installment, the Focus Attacks from the previous game have been removed. The game however features the "V-Gauge", which increases as the player receives attacks from his rival and adds three new techniques: V-Skills, V-Reversals, and V-Triggers. V-Skills are special attacks unique to each fighter; for example, Ryu can parry an attack while M. Bison can reflect projectiles, some of which increase "V-Gauge" when successful.
